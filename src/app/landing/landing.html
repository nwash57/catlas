The hard part

Your colony stays private — by design, not by promise.

Mapping feral cats is sensitive. Colonies have been harmed by hoarders, people who dislike cats, and well-meaning strangers who trap and remove. Catlas takes that seriously at the database level.

Non-members see a fuzzed area, never an exact pin. Row-level security in Postgres enforces it — so even a bug in our frontend can't leak a coordinate. Photos have their EXIF GPS stripped on upload, and cat profiles require colony membership to read.

Caretakers can hard-delete their colonies and cats whenever they decide to stop. Your data is yours, and leaving is cheap.
